Note
  The LUMO of bromomethane is a semibonding orbital between "C-Br". The nucleophile tries to approach between "C-Br", but because it is sterically disadvantageous, it approaches "C" or "Br" from the opposite side. At this time, since HOMO is on "Br", repulsion occurs with the nucleophile. Therefore, it is conceivable that the nucleophile approaches "C" and causes a "SN2" nucleophilic substitution reaction.
  In bromobenzene, the positive charge of "Br" is the largest, and in bromomethane, "Br" is closer to the positive charge. Therefore, both compounds have predicted reaction positions on the bromine atom.
  When the reaction is orbital dominant, "C1" and "C6" for bromobenzene and "C1" for bromomethane are predicted as reaction positions from the molecular orbital.
  When the reaction is orbitally dominant, the charge predicts that "Br" for bromobenzene and "Br" for bromomethane are the reaction positions.
  That is, if it is an orbit-dominant reaction, a nucleophilic substitution reaction is predicted, and if it is a charge-dominant reaction, bromine atom extraction is predicted. Therefore, the predicted reaction positions do not match depending on whether the reaction is orbit-dominated or charge-dominated. If they are known in advance, it will be easier to predict.
  If the reaction mechanism is completely unknown, it is advisable to infer whether the reaction is charge-dominated or orbit-dominated from organic-electron-theoretic ideas (hardness / softness of reagents, etc.) and proceed with the calculation. In some cases.
